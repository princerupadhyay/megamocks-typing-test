Top 5 AI Habits Every iPhone User Should Build Right Now

Most iPhone users have powerful AI tools sitting right in their pocket — and use maybe 10% of what those tools can actually do. Top 5 AI Habits Every iPhone User Should Build Right Now is exactly what this is about. At MegaMocks, we cover practical AI tips that make a real difference — and these five habits are as practical as it gets.

These aren't fancy tricks that need a tech degree. They're small changes to how you use your phone. Build these habits and AI stops feeling like an extra step. It starts feeling like a natural part of how you work.

Habit 1: Drag and Drop Into AI

Most people don't know this, but your iPhone already supports drag and drop between apps. It's been there for a while. Almost nobody uses it with AI — and that's a huge missed opportunity.

Here's how it works. Long press on any image or piece of text in a native app. While you're still holding it, use a second finger to swipe over to your AI app — like ChatGPT. Then drop it in. Done.

This sounds small but it cuts out a lot of annoying steps. No more taking a screenshot, going to your camera roll, copying it, switching apps, and pasting. You just drag and drop.

A few real ways this saves time:

  • You come across a complex graph or chart. Long press the image, drag it into ChatGPT, ask a quick question, and get an explanation in seconds.
  • You get a contract PDF in your files app. Drag it straight into ChatGPT and ask about the terms — even while you're on a call.
  • You're looking at a spreadsheet and want it as a CSV. Screenshot it, long press, drag into ChatGPT, type "recreate this as a table and download as CSV," done.

One thing to know: not every app supports drag and drop. Third-party apps like Gmail don't always cooperate. That's where habit two comes in.

Habit 2: Pin Your AI App to the Share Sheet

When drag and drop doesn't work, you need a backup. The best one is pinning your AI app to your iPhone's share sheet so it's always one tap away.

The share sheet is that menu that pops up when you hit the share button in any app. By default, your AI apps probably aren't anywhere near the front. Here's how to fix that:

Open any app and tap share. Scroll all the way right and tap More. Tap Edit, toggle on your AI app (make sure it's installed), hit the plus icon to add it to favorites, drag it right next to AirDrop, and tap Done.

Now every time you hit share anywhere on your phone, your AI is right there. This opens up a lot:

  • Websites — Share a link from Safari or Chrome to ChatGPT and ask it to summarize. ChatGPT can read the page as long as it doesn't require a login.
  • Emails — In Gmail or Apple Mail, go to the print option first, then share to your AI. The email lands as a PDF, ready to process.
  • LinkedIn posts — LinkedIn blocks direct sharing, so open the post in Chrome, take a full-page screenshot, and share that to AI instead.
Pro tip: If you're commuting and can't read, share an article, ask for a summary, and tap the speaker icon to listen. You stay on top of things without staring at your screen.

Habit 3: Use a Prompt Snippet Keyboard

Typing long AI prompts on a phone is slow and annoying. The fix is text replacement — and it works really well for this.

Text replacement lets you type a short keyword and your phone expands it into a full phrase automatically. You've probably seen autocorrect do something like this. The trick is to set it up on purpose for your most-used AI prompts.

Go to Settings, search for Text Replacement, tap the plus icon, type your full prompt as the "Phrase," and set a short keyword as the "Shortcut."

Smart move: use a ZZ prefix for all your prompt shortcuts. It's fast to type and it won't fire accidentally in normal writing.

Three to start with:

  • ZZ-ANAL expands to: "Analyze this excerpt. Share the top five takeaways and a clear, concise, and objective summary." Great for turning a wall of text into quick talking points before a meeting.
  • ZZ-IMG expands to: "Describe what this image is about and extract all text from it so I can copy and paste." Perfect for screenshots, dashboards, and charts people send you.
  • ZZ-LATE expands to: "Detect the language of this text and translate it to English." Useful if you work with people across different languages.

Mac users get a bonus: text replacement syncs across Apple devices. Set it up on your MacBook and the shortcuts appear on your iPhone automatically.

Habit 4: Map the Action Button to Open AI by Voice

If you have an iPhone 15 Pro or newer, you have an Action Button on the side. Most people set it to silent mode and move on. There's a much better use for it.

Map it to open ChatGPT and start voice dictation instantly. One press and you're talking to AI. No unlocking, no hunting for the app, no typing anything.

Setup: go to Settings, search Action Button, scroll to the Shortcut option, search for ChatGPT, and choose Start Dictation — not just "open app." That last part matters. Start Dictation means it opens and immediately starts listening, so you can speak your question right away without any extra taps.

A quick real example: you're in a store looking at two products with no idea which to pick. Press the button and say "which pillow type is better for side sleepers?" ChatGPT searches and answers in seconds. No typing required.

If you already had something else mapped to the Action Button — like Do Not Disturb — move that to the Back Tap setting instead. Go to Settings, search Back Tap, and set a double tap to a shortcut for whatever you moved. You keep both features, nothing gets lost.

Habit 5: Build an AI Actions Shortcut

This one takes a few minutes to set up. Once it's done though, it's the most useful habit on this list.

The idea: create a custom shortcut that shows up in your share sheet. When you highlight any text and tap share, this shortcut gives you a menu of pre-built AI actions — things like Quick Summary, Draft a Reply, or Explain Like I'm Five. Pick one, and it automatically grabs your selected text, combines it with the right prompt, copies it to your clipboard, and opens ChatGPT. You tap paste and hit send.

Two examples of how this plays out in real life:

You're reading something confusing — a contract clause, a regulation, anything dense. Highlight it, tap share, pick Explain Like I'm Five, tap paste in ChatGPT, and you get a plain English explanation in seconds.

You get a quick work email while away from your desk. Highlight the body, tap share, pick Draft Reply, tap paste, add your thoughts, send. Done in two minutes.

You can add as many actions as you want. One that's genuinely useful: Analyze Meeting Notes — automatically pulls out action items, owners, and deadlines from any notes you paste in.

Heads up: There's currently a small annoyance where the shortcut can't auto-send text to ChatGPT. You still have to tap paste manually once the app opens. It's a known issue waiting on a fix from OpenAI. Even with that one extra tap, the whole thing is still much faster than doing it by hand.

Why These Habits Actually Stick

Most AI tips sound great but never make it into your daily routine. These five are different because they plug into things you're already doing. You already tap share. You already type on your keyboard. You already take screenshots. These habits just redirect those existing actions toward AI.

That's the whole point. The goal isn't to build some elaborate new system. It's to make AI the natural next step from what you're already doing on your phone. Start With One

Don't try all five today. Pick one.

The easiest starting point is Habit 2 — pinning your AI app to the share sheet. Takes two minutes. You'll use it the same day. Once that becomes automatic, add the prompt snippet keyboard. Then drag and drop. Build from there.
