What Makes Manus Different

ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini — they all work the same way. You type something. They respond. Then you go execute.

Manus does the executing.

It opens tabs. Clicks buttons. Fills out forms. Sends emails. Books calendar events. Posts to Slack. You describe what you want in plain English, and it goes and does it. Think of it like a digital employee that works around the clock, never takes a sick day, and costs $39 a month.

Heads up: When Manus starts working on something, stay in the tool. It's tempting to walk away and come back. Don't. Watch it run. You'll want to see it.

Proof Point #1 — A Full Website in Under 20 Minutes

A few months ago, building a website meant finding a freelancer or an agency. Most people spent $3,000 to $10,000. It took weeks of back-and-forth. Then six months later, a small update meant starting the whole process over.

Someone watched a quick Manus demo one evening. She was not a technical person. That same night, she went home and built her website, set up a registration page, and connected Stripe payments — on her own, start to finish.

Here's how it works. You go to Manus, pick the "Build Website" feature, and just talk to it using the voice button. Tell it what kind of business you have, what pages you need, what style you want, and drop in a reference URL if you have one. It opens a browser, writes the code, builds the pages, and adds copy. You give feedback by talking — it makes changes on the spot.

When you're happy with it, you connect it to a domain and you're live. Under 20 minutes. Used to cost thousands and take weeks.

Proof Point #2 — A Working App With No Developer

Building an app used to cost anywhere from $10,000 to $100,000. Months of development. Bugs. Delays. Features nobody used. Most people gave up before it ever shipped.

Manus builds a working app in under an hour.

You write a simple plan first — who the app is for, what it does, how it flows. Then you go to Manus, pick "Develop Apps," and describe it out loud. Something like: "Build me a client intake app. Clients can create an account, fill out a questionnaire, upload documents, and check their project status."

You can attach sketches or screenshots of apps you like for reference. Manus takes all of it and builds. It gives you a live deployment link when it's done. You can share it with teammates or clients to get feedback right away.

What used to take months and cost tens of thousands now takes less time than eating lunch.

Proof Point #3 — A Month of Content Ideas in 15 Minutes

Most people sit down every week, stare at a blank screen, and spend hours trying to figure out what to post. It's exhausting. And the content ends up inconsistent because the process is inconsistent.

One prompt in Manus fixes that. Use the "Wide Research" feature and tell it something like: "I run a personal training business for busy professionals. Find the top 20 fitness creators on Instagram and YouTube. Look at which posts performed way above their average. Use those patterns to build me a 30-day content calendar for Instagram and LinkedIn — post ideas, hooks, captions, and best posting times."

It goes to the internet, finds what's actually working, and builds your calendar around real data. Every day has a platform, content type, hook, and caption ready to go.

12 to 15 hours of brainstorming every month, down to 15 minutes.

Proof Point #4 — Full Competitor Research in 30 Minutes

Andy Grove, who built Intel, used to tell his team: if you aren't tracking your competitors every week, you're walking across a highway with a blindfold on. Most small businesses have no idea what their competition is doing. That's a big problem.

Manus handles this with one prompt. Use the Wide Research feature. Tell it to look up your top five competitors and pull their pricing, positioning, customer reviews, social media strategy, ad campaigns, and website structure. Ask it to find the top five places where your business can do something different.

It opens each website, scrapes the data, checks reviews, scans ad libraries, and delivers a comparison spreadsheet. Then you can tell it to package the top findings into a clean presentation and send it to someone on your team via Slack.

Used to take 10 to 20 hours. Now it takes 30 minutes.

Proof Point #5 — Find and Reach the Right Candidates While You Sleep

Most people post a job listing and wait. They get flooded with resumes, spend 15 to 25 hours sorting through them, and by the time they reply to anyone — the best candidates are already gone.

Manus flips this. Tell it to create a form that gathers everything it needs to find your ideal candidate. It asks about the role, required skills, location, deal breakers, and salary range. You fill it out. It searches LinkedIn, Indeed, and other sources, ranks candidates by fit, and shows you a list with names, profiles, experience, and a fit score.

Then you tell it to write a personalized outreach message to your top picks and send it. You're not waiting for people to apply. You're going straight to the right people first.

Used to take 25 hours and sometimes $5,000 to $10,000 in recruiter fees. Manus does it in about 45 minutes.

Proof Point #6 — 200 Qualified Leads Found and Emailed in 2 Hours

Lead generation used to need a whole team. People at multiple monitors scraping LinkedIn, copying emails into spreadsheets, grinding for weeks just to find 100 decent names. Soul-crushing work.

With Manus, you can find 200 qualified leads and send the first email to all of them in about two hours. The prompt looks something like this: "Find me 200 e-commerce businesses doing $1 million to $10 million in revenue in the US. For each one, find the CEO or marketing director — their name, email, LinkedIn, and company website. Save it as a spreadsheet."

Manus does all of that. Then you connect your Gmail account and run a second prompt: "Write a personalized three-sentence email for each lead, referencing something specific from their LinkedIn or website." Then you tell it to send. It sends from your actual inbox, as you.

This is the kind of system that used to cost $10,000 to $40,000 a month to outsource. Now it takes two hours. And if you want to know which other tools can pull off something similar, our guide on 5 AI Tools That Replace Hours of Work (And When to Use Each One) is worth a look.

Proof Point #7 — Revive Dead Deals and Get Money Back You Thought Was Gone

Every business has leads that went cold. The conversation was going great. They were excited. Then nothing. Most businesses lose anywhere from 3% to 50% of their deals this way. And the team is usually too busy with new leads to ever go back.

Here's the thing — most of those leads aren't dead. They're just bored of getting lazy follow-up emails. "Just checking in" is the three-word phrase that kills more deals than anything else. It adds zero value. If you want a reply, send them something useful — like an article about a specific problem they mentioned, or news about a competitor. That gets responses.

Manus handles all of this automatically. Connect your CRM or paste in a spreadsheet. Tell it to find every deal with no activity in the last 30 days. For each one, it reads the last conversation, figures out why they likely went cold, finds a relevant article that actually helps them, and writes a personalized re-engagement email. Friendly tone. Not salesy. Then it sends.

30 to 45 minutes a month. That's it. And it can recover $25,000 to $50,000 in deals that were just sitting there waiting.

It stops feeling like a tool and starts feeling like someone on your team.

Yes. Every one of these seven examples proves it.

A year ago, doing all of this would've required a full team and a six-figure budget. Now it's $39 a month and an afternoon.
