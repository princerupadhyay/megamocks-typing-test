How This Tool Works

For each subject, the tool calculates: (questions solved ÷ total questions) × 100. It then shows your coverage as a percentage and a progress bar. Subjects below 50% coverage are flagged as priority areas.

Why PYQs Matter So Much

Previous year questions tell you exactly what the exam has tested before. They show patterns in question types, topic weightage, and difficulty level. A student who has solved 80%+ of PYQs for a subject almost always goes into that exam with more confidence than someone who has only read the textbook.

PYQs are not optional preparation — for most competitive and board exams, they are the most efficient thing you can do.

Study Tips

  • → Don't just solve PYQs — analyse why you got each wrong answer wrong. That's where the real learning is.
  • → Aim for at least 70% PYQ coverage in every subject before your exam.
  • → Past papers predict future patterns. If a topic appears in 5 out of 7 years, assume it'll appear again.
  • → Update this tracker every few days so you can see your progress and stay motivated.
